# Ranked Bibliography for the Paper (most to least important)

**How to read this.** The single most dangerous citation is #1 (Calotoiu et al., ICS 2022): it is our own group's SDFG-lifting predecessor on the same substrate, and if we cannot demonstrate a measured performance residual beyond its built-in restrict and global fast-math, the paper collapses to "ICS 2022 plus more flags." The single strongest differentiator is that we emit many correctness-equivalent variants of one unit across compilers, languages, and precisions, select by measured runtime under a numerical-error oracle that admits FP-reordered variants which exact/bitwise oracles structurally reject, and causally attribute which language-contract feature won — no prior work combines these four axes. Read each entry's **Position** line as the specific modifier that survives against that threat; the recurring load-bearing delta is the four-way combination (cross-compiler × cross-language × per-transform-FP-under-oracle × attribution), and any entry whose delta rests only on aliasing or on global fast-math is a framing trap to avoid.

## Tier 0 — Defines the gap / closest threats (the paper must position head-on)

**1. Calotoiu, Ben-Nun, Kwasniewski, de Fine Licht, Schneider, Schaad, Hoefler, "Lifting C Semantics for Dataflow Optimization", ICS 2022 (10.1145/3524059.3532389; arXiv:2112.11879).** — Our own group; lifts C into the SDFG substrate, statically removes aliasing, emits CPU/GPU/FPGA, applies global -ffast-math and -fno-math-errno on a single compiler (gcc). **Position:** the decisive baseline. SDFG already owns restrict/aliasing removal and -fno-math-errno, so our residual does NOT come from aliasing (table-stakes ICS'22 already captures). It comes from (i) cross-compiler codegen divergence on the same aliasing-clean IR, (ii) per-transform FP association gated by a numerical oracle — a correctness-constrained residual, namely the fraction of global-fast-math speedup that survives per transform where error stays in tolerance, kept precisely where ICS'22's wholesale fast-math must be turned off for correctness (not a raw speed win over global fast-math, which is strictly more permissive), (iii) induction-type/signed-overflow (UB) license, (iv) cross-language contract — all selected by measured runtime with per-feature attribution. Without a measurable residual we collapse to "ICS 2022 + more flags". **Threat:** defines-gap.

**2. Shivam, Nicolau, Veidenbaum, "A Multiple Compiler Framework for Improved Performance" (MCompiler), LCPC 2023, LNCS 14480 pp. 174-194 (10.1007/978-3-032-02436-7_12; precursor arXiv:1905.12755; volume/pages verify against LCPC 2023 proceedings).** — Per-loop-nest cross-compiler arena: compiles each nest with gcc/clang/Polly/Pluto, times each in-application, links the best; ML predictor within 4-8% of search; 1.96x/2.62x geomean. **Position:** the sharpest arena prior and our per-unit cross-compiler axis; it selects only across COMPILERS on the ONE fixed source form, with no style/language-contract variants, no precision axis, no FP-safety gate, and no attribution of which feature won. Our contribution is exactly that residual. Cite as LCPC 2023, not "LCPC'24". **Threat:** defines-gap.

**3. Gong, Chen, Szaday, Wong, Sura, Watkinson, Maleki, Padua, Nicolau, Torrellas, Veidenbaum, "An Empirical Study of the Effect of Source-Level Loop Transformations on Compiler Stability", PACMPL 2(OOPSLA) Art. 126, 2018 (10.1145/3276496).** — Shows semantically-equivalent source variants of a loop yield wildly divergent, compiler-specific performance ("compiler instability"). **Position:** the decisive style-variant prior and our strongest supporting evidence; we differ by (a) generating variants from a lifted semantics-explicit SDFG that varies the LANGUAGE CONTRACT (restrict/alignment/induction-type/reduction-shape/per-transform-FP) rather than hand loop-transforms, (b) causally ATTRIBUTING which feature flips each optimizer, (c) adding a cross-precision axis with an FP-oracle gate. This is a study, not a competing system, hence #3 behind MCompiler. **Threat:** must-cite.

**4. Moses, Chelini, Zhao, Zinenko, "Polygeist: Raising C to Polyhedral MLIR", PACT 2021 pp. 45-59 (10.1109/PACT52795.2021.00011; arXiv:2011.10820).** — Raises C/C++ into polyhedral-amenable MLIR and beats Polly/Pluto on Polybench (seq 2.53x vs 1.41/2.34; par 9.47x vs 3.26/7.54). **Position:** the "lift to optimize" framing threat; Polygeist raises to affine and runs ONE optimization pipeline, whereas we emit many semantic-contract styles across compilers/languages and select by measured correct runtime with attribution. **Threat:** must-cite.

**5. Taneja, Laird, Yan, Musuvathi, Lahiri, "LLM-Vectorizer: LLM-Based Verified Loop Vectorizer", CGO 2025 (10.1145/3696443.3708929; arXiv:2406.04693).** — Multi-agent LLM generates SIMD variants and verifies each by Alive2 bounded translation validation; 1.1x-9.4x over icc/gcc/clang on loops they fail to vectorize. **Position:** the single Tier-0 correctness-GATED variant selector; its Alive2 gate is bitwise/translation-validation and STRUCTURALLY cannot admit the FP-reordering variants that are our whole point, whereas our numerical-oracle gate can. It is single-style, single-language (LLVM IR), no cross-compiler arena, no attribution. **Threat:** must-cite.

**6. Rubio-González, Nguyen, Nguyen, Demmel, Kahan, Sen, Bailey, Hough, Hollerbach, "Precimonious: Tuning Assistant for Floating-Point Precision", SC 2013 (10.1145/2503210.2503296).** — The canonical automatic precision-lowering search that reduces variable precision subject to an error threshold. **Position:** the load-bearing ancestor of our cross-precision axis and the anchor for the precision-gap claim (replacing the unverifiable Kernel-Tuner cite as the head-on reference); it tunes precision as a search knob on one source/compiler with no per-transform association license, no cross-compiler arena, and no attribution — the same delta shape we use elsewhere, but this is the citation a reviewer will reach for first. **Threat:** defines-gap.

## Tier 1 — Core positioning (SDFG substrate, closest non-LLM style-arena, compiler-unreliability evidence, semantics-as-barrier, FP attribution, lifting lineage)

**7. Ben-Nun, de Fine Licht, Ziogas, Schneider, Hoefler, "Stateful Dataflow Multigraphs", SC 2019 (10.1145/3295500.3356173; arXiv:1902.10345).** — Introduces the SDFG data-centric IR and DaCe, separating program definition from optimization and mapping to CPU/GPU/FPGA via graph transformations. **Position:** the substrate our whole system builds on; our contribution is the multi-style emission + measured selection + attribution layer that SDFG enables but does not itself provide. **Threat:** core-positioning.

*Entries 8-11 are the closest non-LLM style-arena — "one semantics, many equivalent shapes, pick fastest" — our exact skeleton minus (cross-compiler × cross-precision × FP-oracle × attribution). This four-way delta is load-bearing and must be argued head-on; these are a quieter danger than the LLM rewriters.*

**8. Steuwer, Fensch, Lindley, Dubach, "Generating Performance Portable Code Using Rewrite Rules", ICFP 2015 pp. 205-217 (10.1145/2784731.2784754); Steuwer, Remmelg, Dubach, "LIFT", CGO 2017 (IEEE; DOI to verify — the previously cited 10.5555/3049832.3049841 is an ACM-DL placeholder id, not a DOI).** — Functional data-parallel IR whose rewrite rules generate many equivalent low-level OpenCL variants, then explore + autotune to hand-tuned performance. **Position:** the strongest functional-IR "one semantics, many shapes, pick fastest" precedent; single language (OpenCL), single-compiler, no cross-precision axis, treats rewrites as exact, attributes nothing beyond the winning rewrite. Correct authors are Steuwer/Fensch/Lindley/Dubach; fix the fabricated 10.5555 DOI before submission. **Threat:** defines-gap.

**9. Kloeckner, "Loo.py: transformation-based code generation for GPUs and CPUs", ARRAY 2014 (10.1145/2627373.2627387).** — Polyhedral-like array data model plus transformation library emitting many equivalent shapes for GPU/CPU from one kernel. **Position:** embodies our motivation almost verbatim but leaves selection AND attribution to the human and targets one backend; our contribution is the automatic measured cross-compiler selection + attribution + FP-safety over exactly this shape space. **Threat:** defines-gap.

**10. Teixeira, Ancourt, Padua, Gropp, "Locus: A System and a Language for Program Optimization", CGO 2019 pp. 217-228 (DOI unverified).** — Orchestrates application of multiple (third-party) transformations to code regions and searches the resulting space for portability. **Position:** the tightest overlap with "apply different transform sets, then search"; selects empirically without attributing which transformation feature caused a compiler's win, and has no cross-precision/FP correctness backstop. **Threat:** defines-gap.

**11. Ihno et al. (Ivanov, Grosser group), "DialEgg: Dialect-Agnostic Equality Saturation for MLIR", CGO 2025 (10.1145/3696443.3708957).** — Generates many correctness-equivalent MLIR variants by equality saturation and extracts the best by a static cost model within one IR. **Position:** pulled out of the umbrella cite because it is our skeleton — "emit many correctness-equivalent variants, select best" — de-fanged only by being static-cost-model / single-IR / single-compiler / no FP gate; our explicit rebuttal is measured cross-compiler-and-cross-language runtime arena versus static-cost eq-sat extraction. **Threat:** defines-gap.

**12. Maleki, Gao, Garzaran, Wong, Padua, "An Evaluation of Vectorizing Compilers", PACT 2011 pp. 372-382 (10.1109/PACT.2011.68).** — On the 151-loop TSVC suite only 45-71% of synthetic loops vectorize (icc 90/xlc 68/gcc 59 of 159), almost no real-app loops. **Position:** quantifies the large residual a single compiler leaves, the headroom our multi-style multi-compiler arena harvests; the baseline number arguing no "lift + one compiler + global fast-math" pipeline captures achievable performance. **Threat:** must-cite (evidence base).

**13. Siso, Armour, Thiyagalingam, "Evaluating Auto-Vectorizing Compilers through Objective Withdrawal of Useful Information", ACM TACO 16(4) Art. 51, 2019 (10.1145/3356842).** — Progressively withholds alignment/aliasing/trip-count info from 5 compilers on modified TSVC across AVX2/AVX-512/KNL/AltiVec; adverse categories reach only ~40% of peak vector performance. **Position:** the inverse of our experiment (they subtract semantic info and watch performance fall; we add it back as explicit IR-derived per-transform license); their per-category, per-compiler deltas are the quantitative backbone for our attribution claim, and this is the correct citation replacing any brief "Callanan TACO'19". **Threat:** must-cite (evidence base).

**14. Rubio-González, Nguyen, Demmel, Kahan, Sen, Bailey, Hough, Iancu, "Floating-Point Precision Tuning Using Blame Analysis", ICSE 2016 (10.1145/2884781.2884850).** — Causally attributes which instructions/variables force high precision, then drives precision lowering from that blame. **Position:** the closest prior to our win-attribution because it is attribution-in-the-FP-domain; distinguish sharply — it attributes a precision REQUIREMENT to variables, whereas we attribute PERFORMANCE wins to language-contract features across compilers, and it has no cross-compiler style arena. **Threat:** must-cite.

**15. Schkufza, Sharma, Aiken, "Stochastic Superoptimization" (STOKE), ASPLOS 2013 (10.1145/2451116.2451150); Sharma, Schkufza, Churchill, Aiken, "Data-Driven Equivalence Checking", OOPSLA 2013 (10.1145/2509136.2509509).** — Searches program variants ranked by a combined correctness+performance objective, with FP equivalence checked by testing under tolerance rather than bitwise proof. **Position:** the closest philosophical relative to our oracle gate and the sharpest contrast with the exact/bitwise oracles of #5/#21; it is single-compiler x86 superoptimization with no cross-language/cross-compiler arena and no feature attribution. **Threat:** must-cite.

**16. rust-lang/rust noalias saga (issues #31681, #54878, #84958; LLVM noalias toggled across <12 to >=12, 2016-2021; engineering evidence, not peer-reviewed).** — Rust's &mut carries a no-alias contract stronger than C restrict; enabling the LLVM noalias attribute repeatedly exposed miscompiles and had to be disabled/re-enabled until LLVM was fixed. **Position:** load-bearing evidence that the no-alias contract is a real, exploited optimization license beyond naive assumptions AND is fragile and compiler-version-dependent — precisely why emitting the same unit under different per-transform license across compilers and picking the fastest CORRECT one is valuable and why the gate is not optional. Pair with an academic anchor (Jung, Stacked/Tree Borrows) if a peer-reviewed cite is required. **Threat:** defines-gap.

DOI now filled. **Threat:** supporting.
