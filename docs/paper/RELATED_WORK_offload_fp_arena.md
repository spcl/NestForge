# The DaCe FP-Gated Cross-Compiler Offload Arena

*A survey-grounded design, with its own limits stated inline.*

This document describes an automatic optimizer and, in the same breath, the prior art that already occupies most of its ground. Every mechanism it uses is borrowed. The contribution is a specific union of those mechanisms plus one measured dataset, and several of its headline guarantees are empirical claims rather than proofs. Both facts are stated where they arise rather than deferred to a limitations section.

---

## 1. The Optimizer, Defined

We define an automatic optimizer, the **DaCe FP-gated cross-compiler offload arena**. It operates on a program lowered to a DaCe Stateful DataFlow multiGraph (SDFG) and produces an optimized executable by generating, measuring, and selectively admitting a space of semantically-related variants of each region. The pipeline has five steps.

**Step 1 — Partition into offload units.** The optimizer partitions the SDFG into *offload units* at an automatically chosen loop-nest granularity. An offload unit is the maximal compute-bearing perfect-ish nest that lies within a single schedule and storage domain: a Map scope (or a fused group of Map scopes) whose iteration space, data residency, and device binding are uniform. This is the SDFG analog of a polyhedral SCoP tile band (PPCG, TACO 2013; Baskaran & Bondhugula, Pluto+CUDA, CC 2010), extended by cutting the unit not only at parallelism and storage boundaries but at **numerical seams** — at write-conflict-resolution (WCR) reduction edges, at cancellation-prone subtractions, and at data-dependent control. We name this the **schedule-storage-numerical (SSN) unit**: a storage/schedule domain is treated as the natural floating-point scoping domain, so a reduction sub-band and a pointwise sub-band become distinct units that can carry different floating-point semantics.

This granularity idea is not unoccupied. **HPAC-Offload (SC 2023, Parasyris et al.)** already ties automatic offload-region granularity to numerical approximation under an accuracy/performance harness. Our residual claim over HPAC-Offload is narrow and stated as such in §2 and §5: a per-transform *compiler-flag* lattice rather than a fixed set of approximation techniques, cross-compiler co-generation, and an automatic gate rather than a user-supplied error budget.

**Step 2 — Generate a variant space.** For each unit the optimizer emits a space of variants — semantically equivalent up to floating-point reassociation and precision — from the *same* data-centric IR, across the axes

$$\{\text{compiler} \times \text{language} \times \text{CPU/GPU backend} \times \text{vector length} \times \text{emission strategy} \times \text{precision} \times \text{FP-transform lattice point}\}.$$

The CPU is treated as one accelerator target among GPUs under a **uniform device abstraction** (the execution-space model of Kokkos, JPDC 2014; the uniform Processor model of Realm, PACT 2014): a unit is bound to a device, and the CPU is a peer device carrying its own sub-space of compiler, vector-length, and FP-transform variants (Alpaka, IPDPSW 2016, shows the CPU side is itself multi-variant). Measured CPU/GPU work-partitioning by curve-fitting runs is older still (Qilin, MICRO 2009).

*On the "language" axis, honestly.* DaCe emits C++/CUDA-C++. Two emitted sources that both lower through the same LLVM backend produce no semantically distinct floating-point variant; only a genuinely different frontend/backend (e.g. gfortran vs clang) yields variance beyond the compiler axis, and that variance largely collapses into the compiler axis itself. We therefore treat cross-language as a *partial* capability of our own system (§3), not a full one.

**Step 3 — Measure runtime and error.** Every variant is measured for both wall-clock runtime and numerical error against a fixed reproducible reference evaluated at the unit boundary. Error is a first-class measured quantity, not a modeled one — the point of departure from analytical profitability tools (GROPHECY, SC 2011; Intel Advisor Offload Modeling).

The reference must be defined carefully for a unit containing a reduction. numpy's own summation is pairwise, so a naive "numpy oracle" is itself association-order-dependent and cannot serve as ground truth for a reduction whose whole question is order sensitivity. The reference is therefore a fixed, reproducible summation (ReproBLAS-style deterministic reduction, Demmel & Nguyen, ARITH 2013) with an explicitly stated tolerance semantics per unit, not "whatever numpy returns." For non-reduction units a bit-exact numpy evaluation suffices.

**Step 4 — Gate FP-semantics-changing variants.** A variant that changes floating-point semantics is *admitted* only where a per-transform license certifies safety. The license is resolved separately for each independently-gated axis (see §4.1 for the full axis set), using static idiom detection on the SDFG, condition-number tiers, and, where available, sound static error bounds. Dynamic sampling (Monte-Carlo Arithmetic / shadow execution) may only *veto*, never license — this resolves a contradiction discussed in §4.2. The gate concept itself — admit an accuracy-changing transform only under a static safety analysis, with an asymmetric cost of being wrong — is the founding premise of approximate computing (Rely, OOPSLA 2013; Chisel, OOPSLA 2014; Green, PLDI 2010). Our contribution is the application of that premise to fast-math transforms on a dataflow IR, not the premise.

The gate is **sound-for-danger by construction only to the extent its idiom library is complete**, which it is not (§4.2). We therefore report a *measured* false-enable rate under a stated input distribution and a stated, enumerated idiom-library coverage, rather than claiming soundness as a theorem.

**Step 5 — Select and link.** For each unit the optimizer selects the fastest *admitted* variant; the union of per-unit winners is linked into one program, with region-resident data and early-alloc/late-copyback transfer hoisting (OpenACC `kernels` semantics; Par4All, IMPACT 2012) so per-unit device choices do not thrash host/device copies.

**Per-unit selection objective.** Let $u$ range over offload units and $V(u)$ over the variant space of unit $u$. Let $t(v)$ be measured runtime, $e(v)$ measured error versus the reference, and $\tau_u$ the per-unit tolerance. Let $\mathrm{lic}(v) \in \{0,1\}$ be the conjunction, over the independently-gated transform axes, of the **static** license predicate (recognized safe idiom, satisfied condition-number tier, or sound static bound). The per-unit winner is

$$v^\star(u) \;=\; \arg\min_{v \in V(u)} \; t(v) \quad \text{subject to} \quad \mathrm{lic}(v) = 1 \;\wedge\; e(v) \le \tau_u,$$

with ties broken toward the lowest lattice point (fewest enabled FP transforms). The measured error $e(v)\le\tau_u$ is a *necessary veto* condition, not a license: a variant with small sampled error is still refused unless $\mathrm{lic}(v)=1$. This is the resolution of the §1-vs-§4.2 dilemma — dynamic error can only remove variants, never add them. The optimized program is $\bigcup_u v^\star(u)$.

The design consequence is explicit and is itself a finding, not a failure: because licensing is static and the licensable set is small, the admitted FP-changing set shrinks, and the recoverable FP-changing speedup may be modest. §5 states why that outcome is publishable either way.

## 2. Related Work

**Offload granularity tied to numerical error — already occupied.** The framing "no system chooses offload granularity by numerical error" is false, and we retire it here rather than let a reviewer do so. **HPAC-Offload (SC 2023)** couples offload-region granularity to numerical approximation with an accuracy/performance harness. It does not do a per-transform compiler-flag lattice, cross-compiler co-generation, or an automatic (non-user-budget) gate, so it does not close our opening — but it occupies three of our pillars (automatic granularity, numerical-error measurement, accuracy/perf selection) and is the sharpest single threat. Everything below is residual to it.

**Accelerator offload-granularity selection.** PPCG (TACO 2013) and the Pluto-based C-to-CUDA compiler (Baskaran & Bondhugula, CC 2010) fix the offload unit as the outermost parallel tile band inside a static-control region; imperfect nests yield multiple kernels. Mint (ICS 2011) bakes three hard-coded granularity tiers into stencil annotations. Par4All (IMPACT 2012) uses convex array-region footprints for offloadability and interprocedural transfer hoisting; DawnCC (TACO 2017) auto-annotates loops after symbolic range analysis and pointer disambiguation. Granularity as a *searched* knob appears in the kernel fusion/decomposition work of Mishra, Malik & Chapman (CGO 2019) with an adaptive analytical cost model, and directive systems expose the coarsening knob (OpenMP `collapse`, OpenACC descriptive `kernels`). Within our substrate, DaCe's GPUTransformSDFG (SC 2019) turns a Map scope into one CUDA kernel greedily. None but HPAC-Offload chooses granularity by numerical error; the SSN unit additionally ties the storage/schedule boundary to a floating-point-safety boundary.

**CPU-as-accelerator and uniform-device models.** Kokkos (JPDC 2014) and RAJA (P3HPC 2019) parameterize a loop body by execution/memory space or policy; SYCL/oneAPI DPC++ (Khronos 2020) binds one SPIR-V kernel to a selected device; OpenCL (2009) established device enumeration by type; OpenACC `-acc=multicore` (NVIDIA HPC SDK, 2018) compiles one source to CPU or GPU by flag; OpenMP `target` (4.0, 2013) treats CPU as host fallback. Task runtimes bind a task to a memory/place level (Sequoia, SC 2006; Hierarchical Place Trees, LCPC 2009), model CPU/GPU/OpenMP pools as uniform Processor kinds with a per-task variant registry (Legion/Realm, SC 2012; PACT 2014), or attach multiple per-device implementations to one logical task (StarPU, CCPE 2011; OmpSs/Nanos++, IPDPS 2013; IRIS, HPEC 2021; PaRSEC, CiSE 2013). Qilin (MICRO 2009) fits a CPU+GPU partition by measurement. Alpaka (IPDPSW 2016) and occa (2014) show the CPU side is itself multi-variant. All bind placement statically or by a time-and-locality scheduler, all assume registered variants are bit-equivalent; none measures numerical error, exposes a per-transform FP lattice, or treats FP semantics as part of placement.

**Empirical variant autotuning.** FFTW (Proc. IEEE 2005) and ATLAS (SC 1998) empirically pick codelet/SIMD plans; PetaBricks (PLDI 2009) autotunes algorithmic choice with variable-accuracy targets; Halide (PLDI 2013) autoschedules; TVM/Ansor (OSDI 2018/2020) benchmark-and-cache the fastest schedule per operator across CPU and GPU; OpenTuner (PACT 2014) is a generic ensemble autotuner that can already search any flag space, *including `-ffast-math` sub-flags per region*; BaCO (ASPLOS 2023) is a portable Bayesian autotuner; MCompiler (LCPC 2024) selects the best compiler per loop nest and links winners via an ML predictor. Predictive CPU-versus-GPU placement (Grewe & O'Boyle, CC 2011/CGO 2013) rounds this out. All optimize runtime (and sometimes energy); PetaBricks' accuracy is algorithmic, not an FP-transform; none carries a per-variant error-versus-reference signal or a numerical admissibility gate.

**Per-region fast-math flag selection — not new.** Selective, per-region fast-math already ships commercially (Codee / Appentra) and has been studied as *critical fast-math flag selection* (ASE 2023). OpenTuner can search fast-math sub-flag spaces per region today. Contribution (A) must therefore be framed as *per-transform independent gating measured against a reproducible oracle*, not "per-region fast-math," which exists.

**Cross-compiler FP divergence.** FLiT (IISWC 2017) and FLiT-bisect (HPDC 2019) search compiler × fast-math-flag combinations and detect result *divergence*, and supply the empirical priors this document uses (below). FLiT is cross-compiler and carries a divergence signal, but that signal is not oracle error and FLiT selects for reproducibility, not runtime.

**Precision and FP-error-aware optimization.** Precimonious (SC 2013) and HiFPTuner (ISSTA 2018) delta-debug per-variable precision under an error threshold; ADAPT (SC 2018) and Chef-FP (2023) use automatic differentiation for per-variable sensitivity; GPUMixer (ISC 2019) and AMPT-GA (ICS 2019) tune GPU precision with static Fast-Imprecise-Sets and casting-aware cost; PrecTuner (CGO 2023) and FPLearner (FSE 2024) add model-guided and learned search; the Kernel Tuner accuracy-aware extension (TPDS 2026) jointly measures runtime and error while tuning precision and vector width per kernel; OpTuner (PLDI 2022) allocates a global error budget across math-function call sites by integer linear programming; Carson & Higham's three-precision iterative refinement (SIAM 2017–2018) supplies condition-number ceilings. The Cost of Soundness study (OOPSLA 2025) characterizes sound-versus-unsound error estimation and the false-enable failure mode. Each tunes precision (or math-function choice) alone; none resolves reassociation, FMA-contraction, no-signed-zero, and flush-to-zero as independent gated axes, none is cross-compiler or cross-language, none binds precision to an automatically chosen offload unit under a uniform CPU/GPU abstraction.

**Approximate computing with a safety analysis — the gate's ancestry.** The gate concept is roughly a decade old. Green (PLDI 2010) calibrates approximate versions against a QoS monitor; EnerJ (PLDI 2011) types approximate data; loop perforation (Sidiroglou-Douskos et al., FSE 2011) drops iterations under accuracy monitoring; Rely (OOPSLA 2013) and Chisel (OOPSLA 2014) perform *sound static reliability/accuracy analysis to gate accuracy-changing transforms*. That is precisely the "admit only under a static certificate, with an asymmetric cost of being wrong" premise we build on. Our differentiation is narrow and honest: soundness-for-danger applied to fast-math transforms (reassoc/contract/nsz/ftz/precision) on a dataflow IR, with cross-compiler variant co-generation.

**Numerical-stability detection and selective fast-math.** The LRPD test (PLDI 1995) recognizes reductions and privatizable arrays at run time. Herbie (PLDI 2015) localizes and rewrites expression error; Herbgrind (PLDI 2018) roots-causes error by shadow execution; EXPLANIFLOAT (ARITH 2025) uses condition numbers as a cheap oracle. Verificarlo (ARITH 2016) and Verrou (2018) inject Monte-Carlo rounding and detect unstable branches; CADNA implements discrete stochastic arithmetic; NSan (CC 2021) runs a doubled-precision shadow one-to-four orders of magnitude faster than prior tools; FPChecker and Lam et al. (PARCO 2012) flag cancellation and exceptions. Sound static tools — FPTaylor (FM 2015), Daisy/Rosa (TACAS 2018) — bound worst-case round-off for straight-line kernels over known input boxes, the only class that can *license* a transform rather than merely veto it. ReproBLAS / reproducible summation (Demmel & Nguyen, ARITH 2013) supplies an order-independent reduction reference. The canonical danger taxonomy (Byrne, "Beware of fast-math", 2020) ranks finite-math, FTZ-in-shared-library, and associative-math as the most dangerous sub-flags. LLVM decomposes fast-math into orthogonal per-instruction flags {nnan, ninf, nsz, arcp, contract, afn, reassoc}. All of this is a detector, an accuracy rewriter, or a set of global flags; none turns per-transform detection into a per-unit admit/veto decision driving cross-compiler, cross-precision variant selection.

**Loop-nest and polyhedral optimizers and their associativity assumption.** Polly (IMPACT 2011) parallelizes reductions, implicitly reassociating; Pluto/PPCG and the LLVM/AArch64 vectorizers require `reassoc`+`nsz` to vectorize an FP reduction, or emit an ordered reduction; the transform study of Gong et al. (OOPSLA 2018) measures source-level transform variants across compilers; Exo (PLDI 2022) is deliberately semantics-preserving. Optimizers either silently assume associativity or refuse any FP-changing rewrite. Neither measures the error the reordering causes nor admits an FP-changing variant under a per-transform certificate — the middle ground the arena occupies.

Reading the table by column, most individual capabilities are well covered. Automatic offload-unit selection (**OU**) is solved by the polyhedral line, by MCompiler at loop-nest granularity, and — coupled to numerical error — by HPAC-Offload. CPU-as-peer-device (**CPU**) is mature (Kokkos, SYCL, Legion/Realm, StarPU, IRIS, Qilin). Empirical measure-and-select (**SEL**) is old and robust. The precision-tuning cluster (Precimonious, GPUMixer, AMPT-GA, PrecTuner, Kernel Tuner, HPAC-Offload) jointly occupies **LP**, **ERR**, and **GATE**; single-IR multi-backend (**1IR**) is delivered by SYCL, Halide, TVM. Sound gating of accuracy-changing transforms (**GATE=Y**) is held by Rely/Chisel a decade before us. No column is empty in the prior art, and our own GATE is only `P`.

The scarcity is in the *combinations*. Cross-compiler (**XC**) is held by MCompiler (perf-only), FLiT (divergence, not oracle error), and partially Codee/OpenTuner. Cross-language (**XL**) is genuinely thin, which is why OURS is `P` there and no prior row claims it — the axis adds little beyond **XC** and is not load-bearing. Full per-transform FP resolution (**FPT=Y**) — resolving contraction, reassociation, no-signed-zero, flush-to-zero, finite-math, and precision as independent gated axes rather than a coarse fast-math toggle or precision-only search — is at best partial in every prior row.

The load-bearing intersection is **ERR ∧ XC**. With FLiT in the table, the clean claim "no row carries ERR ∧ XC" weakens to the still-defensible "**no row carries *oracle*-ERR ∧ XC**": FLiT is cross-compiler but its ERR is divergence, not error against a reproducible reference. The systems that measure oracle error (Precimonious, GPUMixer, Kernel Tuner, OpTuner, HPAC-Offload, Verificarlo) are single-compiler and mostly single-device; the systems that are cross-compiler (MCompiler, FLiT) carry no oracle error. OURS is the only row holding **oracle-ERR ∧ XC** together with automatic offload-unit selection, a uniform CPU/GPU device, and per-transform FP resolution. That intersection — not any single primitive — is the contribution, and the honest matrix (OURS at `P` on GATE and XL, with Kernel Tuner and MCompiler adjacent) makes the point without the reverse-engineered-uniqueness smell of an all-`Y` row.

## 4. Embrace & Improve

The reusable pieces are clear. **Adopt** the SCoP-band / Map-scope offload unit (PPCG, DaCe) and make granularity a searched variable via MapFusion / map-split (CGO 2019; OpenMP `collapse`); **improve** it by cutting at WCR-reduction, cancellation, and data-dependent-control seams — while conceding HPAC-Offload already cuts granularity by numerical approximation, so the improvement is the *per-transform-flag* refinement, not the idea of numerically-aware granularity. **Adopt** the "unit → registered variants → selector" data model of StarPU, OmpSs, Legion; **improve** it by inserting a per-transform admission filter *before* the time-only selector — a filter whose intellectual ancestry is Rely/Chisel/Green, which we cite rather than claim. **Adopt** OpTuner's error-Taylor-plus-ILP budget allocation (PLDI 2022); **improve** it by allocating across offload units and lattice points rather than math-function call sites. **Adopt** the CATBench/Bencher packaging (containers plus released surrogate models); **improve** it by adding the per-unit error label those suites omit. Two subsections carry the substantive design.

### 4.1 FP-Flag Levels

The DOC's "four axes" framing was incoherent: it named {reassociation, FMA-contraction, flush-to-zero, low-precision} but the lattice then introduced reciprocal/`arcp`, `afn`, and folded `nsz` into reassociation — six or seven transforms, not four. We fix this by stating the *actual* independently-gated axis set, mapped onto LLVM's per-instruction fast-math flags plus denormal and precision mechanisms:

| Axis | LLVM FMF / mechanism | Danger surface (why it is separately gated) |
|---|---|---|
| **contract** (FMA) | `contract`; `--fmad`, `__fmaf_rn` | Changes results/reproducibility; flips sign in `a*b−c*d`; breaks FMA-free `TwoProduct` and *some* compensated products depend on a specific FMA choice |
| **reassoc** | `reassoc` | Deletes Kahan/Neumaier compensation; reorders reductions |
| **nsz** (no-signed-zero) | `nsz` | *Independent* of reassoc: `signbit`/`copysign`, `atan2`, complex `log`/`sqrt` branch cuts, `1/x` giving `±inf` |
| **finite-math** | `nnan`, `ninf` | Deletes `isnan`/`isinf` guards → control-flow change; ranked most dangerous by Byrne (2020) |
| **reciprocal / division precision** | `arcp`; `--prec-div`, `__fdiv_rn` | `1/x` and `a/b` lose last-ulp accuracy |
| **ftz / daz** | MXCSR FTZ/DAZ; `--ftz` | Process/thread-global denormal flush; poisons neighbours if set by linking |
| **precision (afn / retype)** | `afn` + retyped SDFG; fp32→tf32/bf16/fp16/fp8 | Backward-error scales with κ; only safe under a condition-number tier |

Two consequences of stating the axes honestly:

- **`nsz` is its own axis, not a rider on reassoc.** Bundling them (as the DOC's "L2 = reassoc + nsz" did) silently enables signed-zero danger on any unit touching `copysign`/`atan2`/`1/x`, contradicting the independent-gating claim. It is gated separately.
- **finite-math (`nnan`/`ninf`) is an explicit gated axis with a hard gate**, not an afterthought in the detector. It is the most dangerous sub-flag and is denied on any unit whose control flow can observe a NaN/Inf.

**Poset, not ladder.** The transforms form a product poset $\{0,1\}^7 \times \text{precision-levels}$, and the best safe point is frequently *off* any linear ladder — e.g. low precision without reassoc, or FTZ without contraction. We therefore search the poset with a bounded, gate-pruned exploration, and we do not claim a cheap linear climb reaches all useful points. A default *ordering heuristic* (contract, then reassoc, then reciprocal, then precision, with nsz/ftz/finite-math admitted only under their own gates) seeds the search, but the search is over the poset and its cost is $O(2^k)$ in the worst case, pruned aggressively by the roofline (below) and by early gate denials. The earlier "bounded upward climb" framing undersold this and is corrected.

**Per-toolchain realization, with the pitfalls stated:**

- **clang / LLVM.** `-ffp-contract={off,on,fast}` (contract); `-fassociative-math` or `#pragma clang fp reassociate(on)` (reassoc); `-fno-signed-zeros` (nsz); `-ffinite-math-only` (finite-math); `-freciprocal-math` (reciprocal); `-fapprox-func` + retyped SDFG (precision). *Caveat:* region-scoped `#pragma clang fp reassociate` sets FMF on lexical ops but the loop-vectorizer keys reduction handling off the recurrence phi, so the pragma frequently does **not** produce the fast vectorized reduction that TU-scope `-ffast-math` gives. For the single most important case — reductions — the pragma is insufficient, so compiling the unit as its own translation unit becomes the *common* path, not the exception. That fallback loses cross-unit inlining, which for small units can erase the target speedup. This is a real, costed design problem, not a non-issue, and it is measured, not assumed away.
- **gcc.** `-ffp-contract=fast` (contract); `-fassociative-math` (reassoc) — but GCC requires `-fno-signed-zeros` + `-fno-trapping-math` to enable it, so **on GCC reassoc cannot be enabled without nsz**, and the ideal of fully orthogonal per-axis gating is not realizable on this toolchain. We record that as a toolchain limit, not a solved problem; per-function `__attribute__((optimize(...)))` gives unit scoping.
- **icx / ifx.** `-fp-model precise` as the L0 baseline; `fast=1`/`fast=2` for relaxed points. *Correction:* the classic HPDC-2019 "49.8% of Intel configs result-changing versus 1.8% for clang" figure was measured on **icpc 18** (EDG + proprietary backend), not the LLVM-based icx/ifx, which have materially different fast-math defaults. We do not use the icpc number to characterize icx; we re-measure icx/ifx defaults directly and request the strict baseline explicitly regardless.
- **nvc / nvcc (GPU).** `--fmad`, `--ftz`, `--prec-div`, `--prec-sqrt` are **whole-compilation** flags, so "the same lattice drives the GPU backend per unit" is *false* unless each kernel is a separate `.cu` compilation unit — which loses cross-kernel optimization and is costed as such. The correct per-op GPU mechanism is **intrinsics** — `__fadd_rn`, `__fmul_rn`, `__fmaf_rn`, `__fdiv_rn` for L0-strict per-op semantics, and the `_rz`/relaxed variants for a per-op relaxation — emitted directly by the codegen. The GPU lattice is realized through intrinsic selection per emitted op, with whole-compilation flags used only for units compiled as standalone `.cu` files.

**Flush-to-zero is special and the naive mechanism is wrong.** FTZ/DAZ is a per-thread MXCSR side effect (the `crtfastmath.o` that `-ffast-math` links), not an instruction bit. It must never be enabled by linking. The DOC's "bracket the unit with MXCSR save-set-restore" is *incorrect for parallel CPU units*: MXCSR is per-thread, so setting it in the master thread does nothing on an OpenMP team — exactly the units that make the CPU an accelerator. The correct mechanism sets and restores MXCSR **inside each worker thread of the parallel region**, wrapped in compiler barriers (or `volatile` asm) so FP ops are not reordered across the writes, or compiles the FTZ unit as a separate TU that sets the mode per-thread at entry. Both cost something and both are measured.

**How a lattice point is chosen per unit.** (i) *Prune:* units the per-unit roofline (CACM 2009) marks memory-bound cannot benefit from FP relaxation, so they stay at the strict baseline and are never risked. (ii) *Search:* for compute-bound survivors, explore the gate-pruned poset, admitting a point only if the per-transform *static* license (§4.2) certifies every newly-enabled axis, the measured reference error stays under $\tau_u$, and the measured runtime improves. (iii) *Stop and tie-break* toward the fewest transforms, encoding the FLiT/HPDC-2019 empirical prior that the fastest bit-reproducible build usually wins. For coupled units the greedy search is replaced by an ILP over a program-level error budget in the OpTuner style. The license can only *deny* a point, never add one; dynamic error can only deny; only a static license admits.

### 4.2 Danger / Safety Pattern Detection

The detector runs on the SDFG, which exposes reductions (WCR edges), loop-carried dependences, memlet subsets, and tolerance-symbol comparisons as first-class objects, so idiom detection is structural, not syntactic. It is a three-tier escalation. Its guiding invariant is stated as an *empirical, scoped* property, not a theorem: **the measured false-enable rate is held near zero under a stated input distribution and a stated, enumerated idiom-library coverage.** We do not claim soundness-for-danger as delivered, because it is not — the reasons follow, and they change the default.

**The default is dangerous, not safe.** The DOC's fatal flaw was defaulting *unrecognized reductions to safe* while hard-denying only *recognized* compensated summation. A compensated sum the matcher misses (Neumaier, Klein, Kahan-Babuška, pairwise, blocked, spread across iterations, or hidden behind a helper) would then fall through to "safe" and get reassociation **enabled** — the exact catastrophic false-enable the system exists to prevent. A single false-negative in idiom recognition would flip deny→enable. We therefore **default every unrecognized reduction to dangerous** (strict baseline, reassoc denied). Reassociation on a reduction is licensed only when the reduction is *positively* recognized as a plain, uncompensated accumulation *and* its conditioning is bounded (below). This is the only defaulting direction consistent with near-zero false-enable, and it is the direction the DOC did not take.

**The `n·u·κ` bound is not a safety license by itself.** For a plain WCR sum the forward error is bounded by $n\,u\,\kappa$, but $\kappa$ — the condition number of the sum — *is the danger*, and it is large exactly when summands cancel, which is precisely when order matters and Kahan is used. Marking all plain reductions reassoc-safe is unsound unless $\kappa$ is bounded, and $\kappa$ depends on the operand distribution (statically undecidable in general). So reassoc on a recognized plain reduction is licensed only under a *bounded-conditioning* tier: a static range/sign argument that the summands do not cancel (all-nonnegative accumulation, bounded dynamic range), or a runtime κ estimate under a stated tolerance. Absent that, the reduction stays strict.

**Tier 1 — static idiom match.** *Reliably safe (positively recognized):* elementwise maps with no cross-iteration accumulation; short stencils without running sums; plain non-compensated reductions **whose conditioning is bounded** as above. *Reliably dangerous (hard-deny, no dynamic override):*

- **Compensated summation** (Kahan/Neumaier/Klein/Kahan-Babuška): the fingerprint is a running sum plus a correction scalar from an algebraically-zero subtraction chain `t = s + y; c = (t - s) - y` feeding the accumulator. Reassociation provably deletes the compensation, so reassoc and ftz are hard-forbidden. Because a *missed* compensated sum is catastrophic, the library is enumerated and its coverage is reported as a measured number, and anything not positively classified as plain-and-well-conditioned defaults to this dangerous side.
- **The `a*b − c*d` idiom** (2×2 determinant, orientation predicate, discriminant): partial FMA-contraction of one product breaks the exact cancellation, so contraction is hard-forbidden — FMA is *not* uniformly safe, and giving contraction "the most permissive gate" (as the DOC did) is wrong: contraction changes results, can flip the sign of this idiom, and breaks FMA-free compensated products.
- **Data-dependent control on an FP value** (pivot select, argmax, threshold branch): a flipped comparison is a discontinuous, unbounded output change; ftz, reassoc, nsz, and finite-math are forbidden on the compared value.

*Hard cases handled by discrimination:* **Residual/tolerance loops** whose exit tests a reduced scalar against a tolerance are self-correcting for precision but sensitive in iteration count; they get a strict gate for ftz and reassoc (which can cause false convergence or change the exit) and tolerate low precision only within a condition-number ceiling. **Fixed-iteration solvers** lose that self-correction; reassoc across the carried dependence and low precision are dangerous. Distinguishing the two reduces to detecting the *absence* of a data-dependent tolerance exit, which is reliable when the guard is explicit and defeated by obfuscated guards. The consequential misclassification direction is **tolerance→fixed misread the other way — a tolerance loop misclassified as fixed-iteration** can enable ftz and cause false convergence or non-termination; because that direction is unsafe, ambiguous guards default to the tolerance-loop (stricter) treatment, not the fixed one.

**Tier 2 — AD-sensitivity rank, as a heuristic only.** A single reverse-mode pass (ADAPT, SC 2018; Chef-FP, 2023) yields per-site first-order output-error sensitivity for all sites at once. It is used to *rank* which axis a borderline unit tolerates and to *prioritize* the dynamic backstop — never to *skip* it. Reverse-mode AD is a first-order estimate, not a proof, and it is wrong exactly at cancellation, singularities, and branch flips, so it cannot license skipping the Tier-3 check. The word "provably" is dropped: AD is a heuristic ranker.

**Tier 3 — dynamic backstop, veto-only.** For units Tier 1 leaves "unknown," the optimizer runs the dynamic gate on measurement inputs: NSan-style doubled-precision shadow (CC 2021) as the fast default, checked at the unit boundary against the fixed reproducible reference (ReproBLAS-style for reductions, §1); escalating to Verificarlo/Verrou MCA (ARITH 2016) with PB mode for cancellation, RR mode for reassociation, and Verrou coverage-diff for unstable branches; and, for small units with known operand ranges, FPTaylor/Daisy (FM 2015; TACAS 2018) sound bounds. **The dynamic tier can only veto.** A satisfied finite-sample error test does *not* license a transform, because low sampled error does not certify safety on held-out inputs — the failure mode the Cost of Soundness study (OOPSLA 2025) characterizes. Only a sound static bound, a positively-recognized safe idiom with a closed-form error law, or a satisfied condition-number tier licenses enabling. Sound bounds scale only to small straight-line kernels and the idiom set is finite, so the licensable set is genuinely small — and that shrinkage is reported as a finding.

**Condition-number tiers are scoped, not a general law.** "fp16 safe for $\kappa \lesssim 10^4$" is Carson & Higham's *backward-error result for LU-based iterative refinement* (SIAM 2017–2018), not a license for arbitrary low-precision units. It is applied only to units matching the iterative-refinement / residual-solver shape it was proven for; applying it elsewhere would be unsound. Estimating $\kappa$ is itself a runtime, input-dependent computation, and its cost is charged to the unit.

**Headline metric.** The detector is validated with the HPDC-2019 fault-injection methodology (controlled perturbations, reported precision/recall) on a TSVC-style one-hazard-per-unit labeled corpus seeded from FPBench and Herbie's benchmarks. Because a false-enable is a silent wrong answer and a false-disable only costs speed, sampling methods (Herbie, MCA, EXPLANIFLOAT at roughly 80% precision / 96% recall) may only veto. The reported headline is the **recoverable-speedup-under-near-zero-measured-false-enable frontier**: the geomean speedup the arena captures while the empirically-measured false-enable rate is held at (or near) zero on held-out stressed inputs, plotted against tolerance, decomposed into the share from strict-baseline variants versus gate-admitted FP-changing variants. Per the FLiT/HPDC-2019 finding that most applications get their best speed from bit-reproducible builds, the FP-changing share is expected to be modest, and quantifying it — including the honest possibility that it is negligible — is the point.

## 5. The Opening

The gap is an **intersection of columns**, not an unoccupied primitive, and it is narrower than the DOC claimed because HPAC-Offload already sits in part of it. Stated honestly: **per-offload-unit selection of a per-transform fast-math *lattice point*, licensed by a scoped static safety gate with a measured near-zero false-enable rate, chosen from variants co-generated across compilers and CPU/GPU backends from one data-centric IR, and ranked by measured runtime and measured error against a fixed reproducible reference.** Every axis exists somewhere; the conjunction is unoccupied.

**What the closest prior art retires, conceded up front:**

- **HPAC-Offload (SC 2023)** occupies {automatic offload granularity} × {numerical approximation} × {accuracy/perf harness}. It retires the framing "no system chooses offload granularity by numerical error." Residual: per-transform *compiler-flag* lattice (vs a fixed set of approximation techniques), cross-compiler co-generation, and an automatic gate (vs a user error budget).
- **Kernel Tuner accuracy-aware (TPDS 2026)** jointly measures runtime and error and tunes precision, retiring "measure both and select." It is GPU-only, single-compiler, precision-only, on a user-fixed kernel, with no data-centric IR — missing cross-compiler, cross-language, offload-unit, and per-transform-lattice columns.
- **MCompiler (LCPC 2024)** partitions into loop nests, selects the best compiler per nest, and links winners, retiring "cross-compiler per-unit selection" as such. It is perf-only: no error, precision, GPU offload, or FP-transform axis.
- **Rely / Chisel / Green** retire "sound gating of accuracy-changing transforms is new." Residual: fast-math transforms on an SDFG, cross-compiler.
- **Codee / OpenTuner / ASE-2023** retire "per-region fast-math is new." Residual: independent per-transform gating measured against a reproducible oracle.

**Residual novelty, honestly graded:**

- **(A) per-unit per-transform fast-math lattice — incremental.** Per-region fast-math exists; residual is independent per-transform gating measured against a reproducible reference.
- **(B) sound-for-danger gate — incremental and partly undelivered.** Sound gating is a decade old (Rely/Chisel); residual is fast-math transforms on an SDFG. And per §4.2 the soundness is *not* established, so this is demoted from a guarantee to a **measured false-enable rate under a stated input distribution plus an enumerated idiom-library coverage**, together with the static-vs-dynamic decidability split (the fraction of units licensable statically). That coverage split is itself a novel, citable numerical result and is the honest form of the claim.
- **(C) the C5 dataset — substantial, the only pillar with clean air.** A cross-compiler × cross-precision × per-offload-unit dataset pairing measured runtime with measured error against a fixed reproducible reference is held by no cited system: FLiT gives divergence, not oracle error; FPBench has no cross-compiler perf; CATBench/TenSet/GPTune/P3 Explorer are perf-only; HPAC-Offload, Kernel Tuner, and MCompiler each miss at least the cross-compiler-oracle-error-per-unit conjunction. It is buildable from what DaCe already emits, contingent on (i) the reduction-oracle definition (§1, ReproBLAS-style), (ii) hardware-pinned FP behavior, and (iii) TenSet-scale collection plus a downstream learned model.

**Verdict.** The opening is real but it is a *conjunction/integration* opening whose central guarantee the system cannot deliver as a theorem, and whose nearest prior art (HPAC-Offload) it must cite head-on. Integration novelty is the weakest kind at a hostile PC unless the empirical result surprises.

**Strongest defensible framing — lead with the measurement, not the guarantee.** The defensible contribution hiding inside the systems paper is the **dataset paper**: *the first public cross-compiler × cross-precision × per-offload-unit dataset pairing measured runtime with measured error against a fixed reproducible reference*, with the arena as its *generator*. Around it:

1. **Demote "sound-for-danger" to an empirical, scoped claim** — measured false-enable rate under a stated distribution and enumerated coverage, plus the static-vs-dynamic decidability split.
2. **Reframe the headline as descriptive:** a per-unit, cross-compiler, CPU+GPU replication of the FLiT/HPDC-2019 finding — *how much speedup is recoverable from fast-math/precision relaxation once a near-zero measured false-enable rate is demanded, and what fraction requires the unsafe transforms at all?* Both signs of the answer publish.
3. **Ship the resolved §1/§4.2 rule as design:** enabling requires a static license; dynamic error is veto-only and additionally reported as a dataset column. The resulting shrinkage of the enabled set is reported as a finding.

**The one strongest paper is the C1 systems paper reframed as the C5 dataset study:** the DaCe arena partitions into SSN units, searches the per-transform poset under the scoped veto-only gate, links per-unit winners across CPU and GPU from one SDFG, and emits C5 as the primary artifact. Its **make-or-break experiment** is the recoverable-speedup-under-near-zero-false-enable frontier on the 318-kernel corpus (Polybench, NPBench, TSVC, plus FPBench accuracy cores): measure the geomean speedup while the fault-injection-validated false-enable rate is held near zero on held-out stressed inputs, and decompose it into the strict-baseline share versus the gate-admitted FP-changing share. It makes the paper if the FP-changing increment is material over the best bit-reproducible per-unit selection at zero measured false-enable. It is at **serious risk of landing on the null** — the FLiT/HPDC-2019 prior (most applications' best build is bit-reproducible) and the small licensable set (§4.2) both point toward a negligible increment. That null is still publishable *because the dataset and the recoverable-speedup measurement are the contribution regardless of sign*; the guarantee-shaped framing is what the null would kill, which is exactly why the dataset framing is the one to lead with.

## Annotated Bibliography

The load-bearing citations, with why each matters to this design.

- **HPAC-Offload — Parasyris et al., SC 2023.** The sharpest threat and the reason the "granularity by numerical error is unoccupied" claim is false. Couples automatic GPU offload-region granularity to numerical approximation under an accuracy/performance harness. We differentiate on the per-transform compiler-flag lattice, cross-compiler co-generation, and an automatic (non-user-budget) gate. Must be cited head-on.

- **Kernel Tuner, accuracy-aware extension — TPDS 2026.** Closest system on "measure runtime and error, then select." Jointly measures both while tuning precision and vector width per kernel. GPU-only, single-compiler, precision-only, user-fixed kernel — retires our "measure both" claim but not the cross-compiler / offload-unit / per-transform columns.

- **MCompiler — LCPC 2024.** Closest system on cross-compiler per-unit selection: partitions into loop nests, picks the best compiler per nest, links winners. Perf-only, no error/precision/FP axis. The other half of the disjoint-competitor argument.

- **FLiT / FLiT-bisect — IISWC 2017 / HPDC 2019.** Cross-compiler search over fast-math-flag combinations detecting result divergence. Source of the empirical priors used throughout (most builds' best speed is bit-reproducible; Intel-icpc defaults are far more result-changing than clang). Its presence weakens our claim from "no ERR ∧ XC" to "no *oracle*-ERR ∧ XC." The icpc-vs-icx distinction it exposes corrects a factual slip.

- **Rely / Chisel — OOPSLA 2013 / 2014; Green — PLDI 2010.** The intellectual ancestry of the gate: sound static reliability/accuracy analysis admitting accuracy-changing transforms under an asymmetric cost of error. Establishes that the gate *concept* is a decade old; our residual is fast-math transforms on a dataflow IR.

- **Codee / Appentra + critical fast-math flag selection — ASE 2023.** Per-region fast-math flag selection already shipping and studied. Retires "per-region fast-math is new"; forces contribution (A) to be framed as independent per-transform gating against a reproducible oracle.

- **OpTuner — PLDI 2022.** Allocates a global error budget across math-function call sites by ILP. The budget-allocation mechanism we adopt and re-target to offload units and lattice points.

- **Precimonious — SC 2013.** The canonical precision delta-debugger under an error threshold; template for the LP/ERR/GATE cluster and the scoring rule that makes precision-only tuners `FPT=P`.

- **Verificarlo / Verrou — ARITH 2016 / 2018.** Monte-Carlo Arithmetic and unstable-branch detection; the dynamic Tier-3 backstop. Used veto-only, never to license.

- **FPTaylor — FM 2015; Daisy/Rosa — TACAS 2018.** Sound worst-case round-off bounds for small straight-line kernels over known input boxes — the only tools that can *license* a transform rather than veto it, and the reason the licensable set is small.

- **Carson & Higham — SIAM 2017–2018.** Condition-number ceilings for mixed-precision iterative refinement (fp16 for κ≲10⁴, GMRES-IR-shaped for κ≲10⁸). Applied only to units matching the iterative-refinement shape it was proven for, not as a general low-precision law.

- **ReproBLAS / reproducible summation — Demmel & Nguyen, ARITH 2013.** Order-independent deterministic reduction; the fix for the under-specified "numpy oracle," which is itself pairwise and association-order-dependent. The reference instrument for any unit containing a reduction.

- **Byrne, "Beware of fast-math," 2020.** The danger taxonomy ranking finite-math, FTZ-in-shared-library, and associative-math as most dangerous, with the reassociation-deletes-Kahan worked example. The basis for the hard-deny idioms and for treating finite-math and ftz as explicit gated axes.

- **Qilin — MICRO 2009.** Measured CPU+GPU work-partitioning by curve-fitting — the ancestor of measured CPU-as-peer placement, predating Grewe/O'Boyle.

- **PPCG — TACO 2013; DaCe / GPUTransformSDFG — SC 2019; Kokkos — JPDC 2014.** The offload-unit definition (SCoP band / Map scope), the substrate that emits the variants, and the uniform CPU/GPU device abstraction, respectively. The three adopted foundations the arena extends.

- **The Cost of Soundness — OOPSLA 2025.** Characterizes sound-versus-unsound error estimation and the false-enable failure mode directly. The reason dynamic sampling is veto-only and "sound-for-danger" is demoted to a measured, scoped claim.
